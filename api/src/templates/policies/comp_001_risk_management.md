## 1. Purpose

This Risk Management Policy establishes the framework for identifying, assessing, and managing information security and operational risks at [Organization Name]. This policy ensures that risks are understood, prioritized, and addressed in alignment with the organization's risk appetite and business objectives.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:
- All information security risks
- Operational and technology risks
- Third-party and vendor risks
- Compliance and regulatory risks
- All business units, departments, and functions
- All employees involved in risk identification and management

## 3. Policy Statements

### 3.1 Risk Management Framework

#### 3.1.1 Risk Governance
- Executive leadership shall establish risk appetite and tolerance levels
- Risk management responsibilities shall be clearly defined
- Risk management program shall be reviewed annually
- Risk reporting shall be provided to leadership quarterly

#### 3.1.2 Risk Management Process
1. **Identify** - Discover and document risks
2. **Assess** - Evaluate likelihood and impact
3. **Treat** - Select and implement risk treatment
4. **Monitor** - Track risks and treatment effectiveness
5. **Report** - Communicate risk status to stakeholders

### 3.2 Risk Identification

#### 3.2.1 Risk Sources
- Asset and vulnerability assessments
- Threat intelligence
- Internal and external audits
- Incident analysis
- Business process reviews
- Regulatory changes
- Third-party assessments
- Employee reports

#### 3.2.2 Risk Documentation
Each identified risk shall include:
- Unique identifier
- Description of the risk
- Risk category
- Affected assets or processes
- Potential business impact
- Risk owner
- Date identified

### 3.3 Risk Assessment

#### 3.3.1 Assessment Methodology

**Likelihood Scale:**
| Rating | Level | Description |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| 1 | Rare | May occur only in exceptional circumstances |
| 2 | Unlikely | Could occur but not expected |
| 3 | Possible | Might occur at some time |
| 4 | Likely | Will probably occur |
| 5 | Almost Certain | Expected to occur frequently |

**Impact Scale:**
| Rating | Level | Description |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| 1 | Negligible | Minimal impact, easily addressed |
| 2 | Minor | Limited impact, manageable |
| 3 | Moderate | Significant impact, requires attention |
| 4 | Major | Serious impact, significant resources required |
| 5 | Severe | Critical impact, threatens business viability |

#### 3.3.2 Risk Scoring
- Inherent Risk = Likelihood x Impact (before controls)
- Residual Risk = Likelihood x Impact (after controls)
- Risk score ranges: Low (1-6), Medium (7-12), High (13-19), Critical (20-25)

#### 3.3.3 Assessment Frequency
- New risks: Assessed upon identification
- Existing risks: Reassessed at least annually
- High/Critical risks: Reassessed quarterly
- After significant changes or incidents

### 3.4 Risk Treatment

#### 3.4.1 Treatment Options
- **Mitigate**: Implement controls to reduce likelihood or impact
- **Transfer**: Transfer risk through insurance or contracts
- **Avoid**: Eliminate the risk by stopping the risky activity
- **Accept**: Accept the risk with documented approval

#### 3.4.2 Treatment Requirements
- Treatment selection shall be documented with justification
- Control implementation shall have timelines and owners
- Treatment effectiveness shall be validated
- Cost-benefit analysis should inform treatment decisions

#### 3.4.3 Risk Acceptance
- Risk acceptance requires documented business justification
- Risk acceptance authority based on risk level:
  - Low: Manager approval
  - Medium: Director approval
  - High: VP/Executive approval
  - Critical: Executive leadership/Board approval
- Accepted risks shall be reviewed annually

### 3.5 Risk Monitoring

#### 3.5.1 Ongoing Monitoring
- Key risk indicators (KRIs) shall be defined and tracked
- Control effectiveness shall be monitored
- Risk landscape changes shall be identified
- Emerging risks shall be assessed

#### 3.5.2 Risk Register
- A risk register shall be maintained for all identified risks
- Risk register shall be updated at least quarterly
- Risk register shall include treatment status
- Risk register access shall be controlled appropriately

### 3.6 Risk Reporting

#### 3.6.1 Regular Reporting
- Risk status reports shall be provided to leadership quarterly
- High and critical risks shall be reported to executive management
- Risk metrics and trends shall be analyzed and reported
- Audit committee shall receive annual risk summary

#### 3.6.2 Ad-Hoc Reporting
- New critical risks shall be reported immediately
- Significant risk changes shall be escalated
- Risk treatment failures shall be reported promptly

### 3.7 Third-Party Risk

- Third-party risks shall be assessed before engagement
- Ongoing third-party risk monitoring shall be performed
- Critical third parties require enhanced oversight
- Third-party risks shall be included in risk register

## 4. Roles & Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibilities |
|------|-----------------|
| Executive Leadership | Set risk appetite, approve risk strategy, oversee program |
| Risk Manager | Manage risk program, maintain register, report to leadership |
| Risk Owners | Own and manage specific risks, implement treatments |
| IT Security | Assess security risks, recommend treatments |
| Business Units | Identify operational risks, participate in assessments |
| Internal Audit | Independently assess risk management effectiveness |

## 5. Compliance

### 5.1 Monitoring
- Risk register completeness shall be audited annually
- Treatment implementation shall be tracked
- Risk acceptance validity shall be reviewed
- KRIs shall be monitored continuously

### 5.2 Enforcement
- Untreated high/critical risks shall be escalated
- Overdue treatments shall be reported to management
- Risk management policy violations shall be addressed

## 6. Exceptions

- Exceptions to risk assessment requirements must be documented
- Exceptions require approval from Risk Manager
- Exceptions shall be time-limited and reviewed

## 8. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Risk | Potential for loss when a threat exploits a vulnerability |
| Risk Appetite | Level of risk the organization is willing to accept |
| Risk Tolerance | Acceptable variation from risk appetite |
| Inherent Risk | Risk level without any controls in place |
| Residual Risk | Risk remaining after controls are applied |
| KRI | Key Risk Indicator - metric for monitoring risk levels |
