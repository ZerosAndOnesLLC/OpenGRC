## 1. Purpose

This Data Privacy Policy establishes requirements for protecting personal data processed by [Organization Name]. This policy ensures compliance with privacy regulations (GDPR, CCPA, HIPAA, etc.) and demonstrates our commitment to respecting individual privacy rights.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:
- All personal data processed by the organization
- All employees, contractors, and third parties processing personal data
- All systems, applications, and processes handling personal data
- Personal data of customers, employees, vendors, and other individuals
- Data processing activities across all jurisdictions

## 3. Policy Statements

### 3.1 Privacy Principles

#### 3.1.1 Core Principles
- **Lawfulness**: Process data only with valid legal basis
- **Purpose Limitation**: Collect data only for specified, explicit purposes
- **Data Minimization**: Collect only data that is necessary
- **Accuracy**: Keep personal data accurate and up to date
- **Storage Limitation**: Retain data only as long as necessary
- **Integrity & Confidentiality**: Protect data with appropriate security
- **Accountability**: Demonstrate compliance with privacy requirements

#### 3.1.2 Privacy by Design
- Privacy considerations shall be included in system design
- Privacy impact assessments shall be conducted for new processing
- Default settings shall be privacy-protective
- Data protection shall be built into products and services

### 3.2 Legal Basis for Processing

#### 3.2.1 Acceptable Legal Bases
- **Consent**: Individual has given clear consent
- **Contract**: Processing necessary for contract performance
- **Legal Obligation**: Required by law
- **Vital Interests**: Necessary to protect someone's life
- **Public Task**: Necessary for public interest tasks
- **Legitimate Interests**: Necessary for legitimate business interests (with balancing test)

#### 3.2.2 Documentation Requirements
- Legal basis shall be documented for each processing activity
- Records of processing activities shall be maintained
- Consent records shall be retained as evidence

### 3.3 Individual Rights

#### 3.3.1 Supported Rights
- **Right to be Informed**: Provide privacy notices
- **Right of Access**: Provide copies of personal data
- **Right to Rectification**: Correct inaccurate data
- **Right to Erasure**: Delete data upon valid request
- **Right to Restrict Processing**: Limit how data is used
- **Right to Data Portability**: Provide data in portable format
- **Right to Object**: Stop processing in certain situations
- **Rights related to Automated Decision-making**: Human review of automated decisions

#### 3.3.2 Response Requirements
- Acknowledge requests within 5 business days
- Complete requests within 30 days (GDPR) or 45 days (CCPA)
- Verify identity before fulfilling requests
- Document all requests and responses

### 3.4 Privacy Notices

#### 3.4.1 Notice Requirements
- Notices shall be provided at or before data collection
- Notices shall be clear, concise, and understandable
- Notices shall be easily accessible
- Notices shall be updated when processing changes

#### 3.4.2 Notice Contents
- Identity and contact details of the organization
- Purposes of processing
- Legal basis for processing
- Data retention periods
- Individual rights
- Third-party sharing
- International transfers
- Automated decision-making (if applicable)

### 3.5 Data Collection

#### 3.5.1 Collection Requirements
- Collect only data necessary for stated purposes
- Inform individuals about collection at the time of collection
- Obtain appropriate consent when required
- Document the source of personal data

#### 3.5.2 Sensitive Data
Special categories requiring additional protection:
- Racial or ethnic origin
- Political opinions
- Religious or philosophical beliefs
- Trade union membership
- Genetic or biometric data
- Health data
- Sexual orientation
- Criminal history

### 3.6 Data Sharing

#### 3.6.1 Internal Sharing
- Share only with those who have business need
- Apply access controls based on roles
- Log access to sensitive personal data

#### 3.6.2 Third-Party Sharing
- Data Processing Agreements required for processors
- Due diligence on third-party privacy practices
- Contractual restrictions on further sharing
- Notification to individuals of third-party sharing

#### 3.6.3 International Transfers
- Transfers outside origin jurisdiction require safeguards
- Standard Contractual Clauses or equivalent mechanisms
- Document transfer mechanisms used
- Assess laws of destination countries

### 3.7 Data Security

- Personal data shall be protected per Data Classification Policy
- Encryption required for sensitive personal data
- Access controls shall limit who can access personal data
- Security incidents involving personal data require breach assessment

### 3.8 Data Retention

- Personal data shall be retained only as long as necessary
- Retention periods shall be documented
- Data shall be securely deleted when no longer needed
- See Data Retention Policy (PRIV-002) for specific periods

### 3.9 Privacy Assessments

#### 3.9.1 When Required
- New processing activities involving personal data
- Significant changes to existing processing
- High-risk processing activities
- Use of new technologies

#### 3.9.2 Assessment Process
- Describe the processing activity
- Assess necessity and proportionality
- Identify and assess privacy risks
- Identify measures to mitigate risks
- Document decisions and approvals

### 3.10 Training

- Privacy awareness training for all employees
- Specialized training for those handling personal data
- Annual refresher training required
- Training completion tracked and reported

## 4. Roles & Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibilities |
|------|-----------------|
| Privacy Officer/DPO | Oversee privacy program, advise on compliance, handle requests |
| Legal | Provide legal guidance, review contracts, assess regulations |
| IT Security | Implement technical safeguards, respond to breaches |
| Business Units | Implement privacy requirements, conduct assessments |
| All Employees | Follow privacy policies, report concerns |

## 5. Compliance

### 5.1 Monitoring
- Privacy compliance shall be audited annually
- Data subject requests shall be tracked
- Processing activities shall be inventoried
- Training completion shall be monitored

### 5.2 Enforcement
- Privacy violations may result in disciplinary action
- Serious violations may result in termination
- Regulatory violations may result in fines

## 6. Exceptions

- Exceptions must be documented and approved by Privacy Officer
- Legal review required for exceptions
- Exceptions shall be minimized and time-limited
