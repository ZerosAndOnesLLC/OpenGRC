**Category:** Security

## 1. Purpose

This Physical Security Policy establishes requirements for protecting [Organization Name]'s physical assets, facilities, and personnel. This policy ensures that physical access to sensitive areas is controlled and that physical threats are mitigated to protect information systems and business operations.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:
- All organizational facilities including offices, data centers, and remote locations
- All physical access control systems (badges, keys, biometrics)
- Server rooms, network closets, and other sensitive areas
- Visitors, contractors, and other third parties accessing facilities
- Equipment and assets within organizational facilities
- Environmental controls and monitoring systems

## 3. Policy Statements

### 3.1 Facility Security

#### 3.1.1 Access Control
- All facilities shall have controlled access points
- Access control systems shall authenticate individuals before entry
- Access badges shall be uniquely assigned to individuals
- Tailgating/piggybacking is prohibited
- Lost or stolen badges shall be reported and deactivated immediately
- Badge sharing is prohibited

#### 3.1.2 Security Zones
- Facilities shall be divided into security zones based on sensitivity
- Public areas: Reception, visitor waiting areas
- Restricted areas: General office space (employee access required)
- Secure areas: Server rooms, data centers, executive areas (additional authorization required)
- Access to each zone shall require appropriate credentials

#### 3.1.3 Perimeter Security
- Building perimeters shall be secured during non-business hours
- External doors shall be locked and monitored
- Ground-floor windows shall have appropriate security measures
- Adequate lighting shall be maintained around building perimeters
- Security cameras shall monitor entry/exit points

### 3.2 Visitor Management

- All visitors shall sign in at reception
- Visitors shall present government-issued identification
- Visitor badges shall be issued and visibly worn
- Visitors shall be escorted in restricted and secure areas
- Visitor access logs shall be maintained
- Visitor badges shall be returned upon departure
- Expected visitors should be pre-registered by sponsors

### 3.3 Secure Areas

#### 3.3.1 Data Center/Server Room
- Multi-factor authentication required for entry (badge + PIN or biometric)
- Access limited to personnel with documented business need
- All access shall be logged and monitored
- No food, drink, or storage of personal items
- Cameras shall monitor all activity
- Environmental monitoring shall be in place (temperature, humidity, water)

#### 3.3.2 Network Closets
- Access limited to IT personnel
- Closets shall be locked when unattended
- Access logs shall be maintained
- No storage of non-IT equipment

### 3.4 Equipment Security

#### 3.4.1 Asset Protection
- Laptops and portable devices shall be secured when unattended
- Cable locks should be used in shared spaces
- Equipment shall not be left in vehicles
- Workstations shall be positioned to prevent unauthorized viewing
- Privacy screens should be used for sensitive work

#### 3.4.2 Equipment Removal
- Equipment removal requires documented authorization
- Asset tracking systems shall record equipment movement
- Equipment leaving secure areas shall be logged
- Returned equipment shall be verified and logged

### 3.5 Surveillance

- Security cameras shall be deployed at entry/exit points
- Camera footage shall be retained for minimum 90 days
- Cameras shall not be placed in private areas (restrooms, lactation rooms)
- Camera systems shall have redundant storage
- Access to camera footage shall be restricted and logged
- Signage shall notify of camera presence where required by law

### 3.6 Environmental Controls

#### 3.6.1 Fire Safety
- Fire suppression systems shall be installed and maintained
- Fire extinguishers shall be accessible and inspected regularly
- Fire drills shall be conducted at least annually
- Emergency evacuation procedures shall be documented and practiced
- Smoke detectors shall be tested regularly

#### 3.6.2 Environmental Monitoring
- Temperature and humidity shall be monitored in sensitive areas
- Water/leak detection shall be in place near sensitive equipment
- Environmental alerts shall be sent to appropriate personnel
- UPS and backup power shall protect critical systems

### 3.7 Clean Desk

- Sensitive documents shall be secured when not in use
- Workstations shall be locked when unattended
- Whiteboards and flip charts shall be erased after use
- Printers shall not be used as storage for printed documents
- Shred bins shall be available for document destruction

### 3.8 Key Management

- Physical keys shall be inventoried and tracked
- Key issuance requires documented approval
- Lost or stolen keys shall be reported immediately
- Keys shall be returned upon termination or transfer
- Master keys shall be strictly controlled
- Re-keying shall occur when keys are lost or personnel with access depart

## 4. Roles & Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibilities |
|------|-----------------|
| Facilities/Security | Manage access control systems, monitor security, respond to incidents |
| IT Security | Define requirements for secure areas, audit compliance |
| Reception | Manage visitor check-in/check-out, issue visitor badges |
| Managers | Sponsor visitors, ensure team compliance, report security concerns |
| All Personnel | Follow access procedures, report tailgating, maintain clean desks |
| Building Management | Maintain physical infrastructure, support security requirements |

## 5. Compliance

### 5.1 Monitoring
- Access logs shall be reviewed monthly
- Access badges shall be audited quarterly
- Security camera systems shall be tested monthly
- Physical security assessments shall be conducted annually

### 5.2 Enforcement
- Tailgating violations shall be reported and addressed
- Lost badges not reported within 24 hours may result in disciplinary action
- Clean desk violations shall be addressed with responsible parties
- Serious violations may result in access revocation

## 6. Exceptions

- Exceptions require documented business justification
- Temporary exceptions for visitors in secure areas require escort
- Alternative controls must be documented for approved exceptions
- Exceptions require Facilities and IT Security approval

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
