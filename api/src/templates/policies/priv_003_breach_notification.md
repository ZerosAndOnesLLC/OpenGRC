## 1. Purpose

This Data Breach Notification Policy establishes requirements for detecting, assessing, and notifying appropriate parties about data breaches at [Organization Name]. This policy ensures compliance with breach notification laws and protects individuals whose data may have been compromised.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:
- All security incidents involving personal or sensitive data
- All employees, contractors, and third parties
- Data of customers, employees, vendors, and other individuals
- Breaches affecting data regardless of location or system
- All notification obligations under applicable laws

## 3. Policy Statements

### 3.1 Definition of Data Breach

#### 3.1.1 What Constitutes a Breach
A data breach is a security incident that results in:
- Unauthorized access to personal or sensitive data
- Unauthorized disclosure of personal or sensitive data
- Unauthorized acquisition of personal or sensitive data
- Loss of data resulting in potential unauthorized access
- Theft of systems or media containing personal data

#### 3.1.2 Types of Data Triggering Notification
- Personal Identifiable Information (PII)
- Protected Health Information (PHI)
- Payment Card Data (PCI)
- Financial information
- Login credentials
- Sensitive personal data (as defined by applicable law)

### 3.2 Breach Detection and Assessment

#### 3.2.1 Detection Sources
- Security monitoring and alerting
- Employee reports
- Third-party notifications
- Customer complaints
- Audit findings
- Law enforcement notification

#### 3.2.2 Initial Assessment (Within 24 hours)
- Nature and scope of the incident
- Types of data potentially affected
- Number of individuals potentially affected
- Source and cause of the breach
- Whether the breach is ongoing
- Immediate containment actions needed

#### 3.2.3 Risk Assessment
Evaluate the following to determine notification requirements:
- Type and sensitivity of data involved
- Volume of data and number of individuals affected
- Likelihood of harm to individuals
- Whether data was encrypted
- Whether data has been misused
- Identity and intent of unauthorized accessor

### 3.3 Notification Requirements

#### 3.3.1 Regulatory Notification Timelines

| Regulation | Supervisory Authority | Affected Individuals |
|------------|----------------------|---------------------|
| GDPR | 72 hours | Without undue delay |
| HIPAA | 60 days | 60 days |
| CCPA | 72 hours (if applicable) | Without unreasonable delay |
| State Laws | Varies (typically 30-60 days) | Varies |
| PCI DSS | Immediately to card brands | Per payment brand rules |

#### 3.3.2 When Notification is Required
Notification is generally required when:
- Personal data was accessed or acquired
- There is risk of harm to individuals
- Required by applicable law regardless of risk assessment

Notification may not be required when:
- Data was encrypted with secure key management
- Data was rendered unusable
- Risk of harm is minimal (document this assessment)

### 3.4 Notification Process

#### 3.4.1 Internal Escalation
1. Security team assesses and contains the breach
2. Incident is escalated to Privacy Officer and Legal
3. Breach response team is convened
4. Risk assessment is completed
5. Notification decisions are made
6. Executive leadership is briefed

#### 3.4.2 Regulatory/Authority Notification
- Identify applicable regulatory bodies
- Prepare notification with required content
- Submit within required timeframe
- Document submission and communications
- Respond to regulatory inquiries

#### 3.4.3 Individual Notification
- Identify all affected individuals
- Prepare clear, understandable notification
- Use appropriate delivery method
- Provide required information
- Document notifications sent

### 3.5 Notification Content

#### 3.5.1 Required Elements
- Description of the incident
- Types of information involved
- Date(s) of the breach
- Date of discovery
- Steps taken to address the breach
- Steps individuals can take to protect themselves
- Contact information for questions
- Information about credit monitoring (if offered)

#### 3.5.2 Notification Methods
- Direct notification (email, mail) preferred
- Phone notification for urgent situations
- Substitute notification if contact info unavailable:
  - Conspicuous website posting
  - Major media notification
- Method should be appropriate to reach affected individuals

### 3.6 Third-Party Breaches

#### 3.6.1 When Vendors/Processors Experience Breaches
- Contractual notification requirements shall be enforced
- Vendor shall provide breach details promptly
- Assess impact on organizational data
- Coordinate response with vendor
- Organization remains responsible for individual notifications

#### 3.6.2 Notification to Business Partners
- Notify affected business partners/customers
- Provide information about impact to their data/customers
- Coordinate response efforts
- Support their notification efforts as appropriate

### 3.7 Documentation

#### 3.7.1 Breach Log
All breaches shall be logged with:
- Date of breach and discovery
- Description of incident
- Data types and individuals affected
- Risk assessment results
- Notification decisions and rationale
- Notifications sent and dates
- Remediation actions taken

#### 3.7.2 Retention
- Breach records retained for minimum 7 years
- Evidence preserved for potential litigation
- Documentation supports regulatory compliance

### 3.8 Post-Breach Actions

#### 3.8.1 Remediation
- Address root cause of the breach
- Implement additional safeguards
- Update security controls as needed
- Verify remediation effectiveness

#### 3.8.2 Lessons Learned
- Conduct post-incident review
- Document improvement recommendations
- Update policies and procedures
- Enhance training as needed

#### 3.8.3 Support for Affected Individuals
- Provide clear guidance on protective steps
- Offer credit monitoring for sensitive data breaches
- Establish dedicated support channel
- Monitor for misuse of compromised data

### 3.9 Communication Management

#### 3.9.1 Internal Communication
- Keep stakeholders informed during response
- Provide talking points for customer-facing staff
- Executive updates at appropriate intervals

#### 3.9.2 External Communication
- All external statements reviewed by Legal
- Single spokesperson for media inquiries
- Consistent messaging across channels
- Coordinate with PR/Communications team

## 4. Roles & Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibilities |
|------|-----------------|
| Security Team | Detect, contain, and investigate breaches |
| Privacy Officer | Lead breach assessment, determine notification requirements |
| Legal | Advise on legal obligations, review notifications, manage regulatory response |
| Communications | Manage external communications, prepare statements |
| Executive Leadership | Approve notifications, make strategic decisions |
| Customer Support | Handle inquiries from affected individuals |

## 5. Compliance

### 5.1 Monitoring
- Breach response times shall be tracked
- Notification compliance shall be verified
- Breach trends shall be analyzed
- Regulatory interactions shall be documented

### 5.2 Enforcement
- Failure to report potential breaches internally is a serious violation
- Unauthorized external disclosure may result in termination
- Compliance with notification timelines is mandatory

## 6. Exceptions

- No exceptions to regulatory notification requirements
- Notification timing may only be delayed for law enforcement request
- All delays must be documented with legal approval
