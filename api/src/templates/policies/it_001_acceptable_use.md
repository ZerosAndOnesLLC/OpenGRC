**Effective Date:** [To be set]
**Review Date:** [Annual review recommended]

**Category:** IT

## 1. Purpose

This Acceptable Use Policy establishes the acceptable use of [Organization Name]'s information technology resources. This policy protects the organization, employees, and third parties from inappropriate or illegal use of technology while enabling productive work.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:
- All employees, contractors, consultants, and temporary workers
- All IT resources including computers, networks, software, and cloud services
- All uses of organizational IT resources including during and outside business hours
- Personal devices used to access organizational resources (BYOD)
- Internet, email, and communication systems

## 3. Policy Statements

### 3.1 General Use

#### 3.1.1 Authorized Use
- IT resources shall be used primarily for business purposes
- Limited personal use is permitted if it does not interfere with work duties
- Personal use shall not consume significant bandwidth or storage
- Personal use shall not expose the organization to legal liability
- All use shall comply with applicable laws and regulations

#### 3.1.2 User Responsibilities
- Users are responsible for all activity under their accounts
- Users shall protect their credentials and not share accounts
- Users shall lock workstations when unattended
- Users shall report security incidents or suspicious activity
- Users shall comply with all organizational policies

### 3.2 Prohibited Activities

#### 3.2.1 Strictly Prohibited
- Accessing, downloading, or distributing illegal content
- Harassment, discrimination, or hostile communications
- Unauthorized access to systems or data
- Installing unauthorized software
- Circumventing security controls
- Using IT resources for personal financial gain
- Cryptocurrency mining on organizational resources
- Sharing proprietary or confidential information without authorization

#### 3.2.2 Prohibited Without Authorization
- Connecting unauthorized devices to the network
- Running network scanning or security testing tools
- Modifying system configurations
- Installing or using file-sharing applications
- Using organizational email for mass personal communications
- Representing personal views as organizational positions

### 3.3 Email and Communications

#### 3.3.1 Email Use
- Email shall be used professionally and courteously
- Confidential information shall be protected in transit (encryption)
- Mass emails require appropriate authorization
- Personal email accounts shall not be used for business communications
- Auto-forwarding to external addresses is prohibited
- Email signatures shall follow organizational standards

#### 3.3.2 Messaging and Collaboration
- Approved collaboration tools shall be used for business communications
- Sensitive information shall not be shared on unapproved platforms
- Professional conduct applies to all communication channels
- Retention requirements apply to business communications

### 3.4 Internet Use

- Internet use shall primarily support business activities
- Streaming services shall not consume excessive bandwidth
- Downloads shall be limited to work-related materials
- Blocked websites shall not be circumvented
- Personal social media use shall be limited and professional
- Posting organizational information on social media requires approval

### 3.5 Software and Licensing

- Only approved software shall be installed
- Software shall be used in compliance with licensing terms
- Pirated or unlicensed software is prohibited
- Browser extensions require IT approval
- Cloud services require IT approval before use with organizational data

### 3.6 Data Handling

- Data shall be classified and handled according to the Data Classification Policy
- Confidential data shall not be stored on local devices without encryption
- Data shall not be transferred to personal devices or accounts
- Data shall be stored in approved locations
- Data deletion shall follow retention requirements

### 3.7 Mobile Devices

- Mobile devices accessing organizational data shall comply with security requirements
- Devices shall have screen lock enabled
- Devices shall be reported if lost or stolen immediately
- Remote wipe capability must be enabled for devices with organizational data
- Jailbroken or rooted devices shall not access organizational resources

### 3.8 Remote Access

- Remote access shall use approved VPN or secure methods
- Public WiFi shall be used with VPN protection
- Work in public places shall protect screens from observation
- Remote sessions shall be terminated when not in use
- Remote work environments shall be reasonably secure

### 3.9 Monitoring and Privacy

- The organization reserves the right to monitor IT resource use
- Users should have no expectation of privacy when using organizational resources
- Monitoring may include email, internet, file access, and communications
- Monitoring is conducted to protect organizational interests and ensure compliance
- Access to monitoring data is restricted to authorized personnel

## 4. Roles & Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibilities |
|------|-----------------|
| IT Department | Implement technical controls, manage IT resources, respond to violations |
| IT Security | Monitor for violations, investigate incidents, define requirements |
| HR | Enforce policy violations, manage disciplinary actions |
| Managers | Ensure team compliance, report violations, model appropriate behavior |
| All Users | Comply with policy, report violations, use resources responsibly |

## 5. Compliance

### 5.1 Monitoring
- Internet and email use may be monitored
- Software installation attempts are logged
- Access to sensitive data is logged
- Network traffic may be analyzed

### 5.2 Enforcement
- First violations typically result in warning and training
- Repeated violations may result in progressive discipline
- Serious violations may result in immediate termination
- Illegal activities will be reported to authorities

## 6. Exceptions

- Exceptions must be approved by IT and management
- Exceptions shall be documented with business justification
- Exceptions shall be time-limited and reviewed
- Security teams may be exempt for authorized testing
