# Security Awareness Training Policy

**Policy Code:** SEC-007
**Version:** 1.0
**Effective Date:** [To be set]
**Review Date:** [Annual review recommended]
**Owner:** [To be assigned - typically IT Security Manager or HR]
**Category:** Security

## 1. Purpose

This Security Awareness Training Policy establishes requirements for educating [Organization Name]'s workforce about information security risks and responsibilities. This policy ensures that all personnel understand their role in protecting organizational assets and can recognize and respond to security threats.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:
- All employees regardless of position or department
- All contractors, consultants, and temporary workers
- Third parties with access to organizational systems
- Board members and executives with system access
- New hires from their first day of employment

## 3. Policy Statements

### 3.1 Training Requirements

#### 3.1.1 New Hire Training
- Security awareness training shall be completed within 30 days of hire
- Training must be completed before access to production systems
- Training shall cover fundamental security concepts and policies
- New hires shall acknowledge understanding of security responsibilities

#### 3.1.2 Annual Training
- All personnel shall complete annual security awareness training
- Annual training shall be completed within the designated training period
- Training shall be updated to reflect current threats and policy changes
- Training completion shall be tracked and reported

#### 3.1.3 Role-Based Training
- Personnel with elevated privileges shall receive additional training
- Developers shall receive secure coding training
- Personnel handling sensitive data shall receive data protection training
- Incident responders shall receive specialized training
- Managers shall receive training on team security responsibilities

### 3.2 Training Content

#### 3.2.1 Core Topics
- Information security policy overview
- Password and authentication security
- Phishing and social engineering recognition
- Data classification and handling
- Physical security awareness
- Incident reporting procedures
- Clean desk and screen lock policies
- Mobile device security
- Remote work security

#### 3.2.2 Specialized Topics (Role-Based)
- Secure software development (developers)
- Cloud security (cloud administrators)
- Privileged access security (system administrators)
- Data privacy regulations (personnel handling PII)
- Vendor security management (procurement)

### 3.3 Phishing Simulations

- Phishing simulations shall be conducted at least quarterly
- Simulations shall vary in complexity and attack vectors
- Users who click on simulated phishing shall receive immediate education
- Repeat offenders shall receive additional training
- Simulation results shall be tracked and analyzed
- Metrics shall be reported to department managers

### 3.4 Training Delivery

#### 3.4.1 Delivery Methods
- Online learning management system (primary method)
- In-person sessions for specialized training
- Video-based content for accessibility
- Interactive modules with knowledge checks
- Short-form content for ongoing awareness (newsletters, tips)

#### 3.4.2 Accessibility
- Training shall be accessible to all personnel
- Accommodations shall be provided for disabilities
- Training shall be available in languages appropriate for the workforce
- Mobile-friendly options should be available

### 3.5 Ongoing Awareness

- Monthly security awareness communications shall be distributed
- Security tips shall be shared via appropriate channels
- Security awareness materials shall be posted in common areas
- Security events and incidents shall be used as learning opportunities
- Security champions program may be established

### 3.6 Compliance Tracking

- Training completion shall be tracked in a learning management system
- Completion rates shall be reported monthly
- Non-compliance shall be escalated to managers
- Compliance reports shall be available for audit
- Training records shall be retained for three years minimum

## 4. Roles & Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibilities |
|------|-----------------|
| IT Security | Develop training content, conduct simulations, track compliance |
| HR | Integrate training into onboarding, enforce compliance, track completion |
| Managers | Ensure team compliance, reinforce security culture, address issues |
| All Personnel | Complete training, apply knowledge, report suspicious activity |
| Training Team | Maintain LMS, develop content, generate reports |

## 5. Compliance

### 5.1 Monitoring
- Training completion rates shall be tracked by department
- Phishing simulation click rates shall be monitored
- Training effectiveness shall be measured through assessments
- Security incident correlation with training shall be analyzed

### 5.2 Enforcement
- Access may be restricted for personnel who do not complete required training
- Training non-compliance may be reflected in performance reviews
- Repeated phishing simulation failures may result in additional requirements
- Serious or repeated violations may result in disciplinary action

## 6. Exceptions

- Exceptions for training requirements are rarely granted
- Temporary exceptions may be granted for extenuating circumstances
- Alternative training may be provided for accessibility needs
- All exceptions must be documented and approved by IT Security

## 8. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Phishing | Fraudulent attempt to obtain sensitive information through deception |
| Social Engineering | Manipulating people into divulging information or taking actions |
| Security Champion | Employee who promotes security awareness within their team |
| LMS | Learning Management System - platform for delivering and tracking training |
| Clean Desk | Practice of clearing sensitive information from workspace when unattended |
