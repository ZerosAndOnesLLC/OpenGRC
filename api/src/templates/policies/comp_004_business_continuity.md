# Business Continuity Policy

**Policy Code:** COMP-004
**Version:** 1.0
**Effective Date:** [To be set]
**Review Date:** [Annual review recommended]
**Owner:** [To be assigned - typically Business Continuity Manager or COO]
**Category:** Compliance

## 1. Purpose

This Business Continuity Policy establishes requirements for maintaining [Organization Name]'s critical business operations during and after disruptive events. This policy ensures that the organization can respond to and recover from disruptions while minimizing impact to customers, employees, and stakeholders.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:
- All critical business functions and processes
- All information systems supporting critical operations
- All facilities and physical infrastructure
- All employees, contractors, and key third parties
- Natural disasters, technology failures, and other disruptive events

## 3. Policy Statements

### 3.1 Business Continuity Management

#### 3.1.1 Program Requirements
- A business continuity program shall be established and maintained
- Executive sponsorship shall be designated
- Business continuity plans shall be documented
- Plans shall be tested and maintained annually
- Program shall align with organizational risk management

#### 3.1.2 Program Components
- Business Impact Analysis (BIA)
- Risk Assessment
- Business Continuity Plans
- Disaster Recovery Plans
- Crisis Management Plans
- Testing and Exercises
- Training and Awareness

### 3.2 Business Impact Analysis

#### 3.2.1 BIA Requirements
- Critical business functions shall be identified
- Dependencies shall be documented
- Recovery objectives shall be defined
- Resource requirements shall be identified
- BIA shall be updated annually

#### 3.2.2 Recovery Objectives

| Metric | Definition | Typical Values |
|--------|------------|----------------|
| RTO | Recovery Time Objective - Maximum acceptable downtime | 4 hours to 72 hours |
| RPO | Recovery Point Objective - Maximum acceptable data loss | 0 to 24 hours |
| MTPD | Maximum Tolerable Period of Disruption | Based on business impact |

#### 3.2.3 Criticality Classification

| Tier | Classification | RTO | RPO |
|------|---------------|-----|-----|
| 1 | Critical | 4 hours | 1 hour |
| 2 | Essential | 24 hours | 4 hours |
| 3 | Important | 72 hours | 24 hours |
| 4 | Non-Critical | 1 week+ | 24 hours |

### 3.3 Business Continuity Plans

#### 3.3.1 Plan Contents
- Purpose and scope
- Activation criteria and procedures
- Roles and responsibilities
- Contact information
- Recovery procedures
- Resource requirements
- Communication procedures
- Return to normal operations

#### 3.3.2 Plan Requirements
- Plans shall cover all critical functions
- Plans shall be documented and accessible
- Plans shall be reviewed and updated annually
- Plans shall be distributed to key personnel
- Off-site copies shall be maintained

### 3.4 Disaster Recovery

#### 3.4.1 IT Disaster Recovery
- DR plans shall be documented for critical systems
- Recovery procedures shall be step-by-step
- Backup and recovery capabilities shall meet RPO/RTO
- DR sites shall be geographically separated
- DR infrastructure shall be maintained and tested

#### 3.4.2 Data Protection
- Backups shall meet RPO requirements
- Backup integrity shall be verified
- Recovery procedures shall be documented
- Data replication for critical systems

### 3.5 Crisis Management

#### 3.5.1 Crisis Management Team
- Executive leadership representation
- Operations management
- IT leadership
- Legal/Compliance
- Communications/PR
- Human Resources

#### 3.5.2 Crisis Response
- Activation criteria shall be defined
- Command structure shall be established
- Decision-making authority shall be clear
- Communication protocols shall be documented
- Escalation procedures shall be defined

### 3.6 Communication

#### 3.6.1 Internal Communication
- Employee notification procedures
- Status update protocols
- Work-from-home activation
- Return-to-work communication

#### 3.6.2 External Communication
- Customer communication templates
- Partner/vendor notification
- Media/public relations procedures
- Regulatory notification requirements

### 3.7 Testing and Exercises

#### 3.7.1 Testing Requirements
- Plans shall be tested at least annually
- Testing scope shall include all critical functions over time
- Test results shall be documented
- Gaps shall be addressed and retested

#### 3.7.2 Exercise Types
- **Tabletop**: Discussion-based scenario walkthrough
- **Walkthrough**: Step-by-step plan verification
- **Simulation**: Simulated incident response
- **Full Exercise**: Actual activation of recovery capabilities

### 3.8 Third-Party Considerations

- Critical vendor continuity capabilities shall be assessed
- Vendor recovery commitments shall be contractual
- Alternative vendors shall be identified for critical services
- Vendor dependencies shall be documented

### 3.9 Alternate Work Arrangements

- Remote work capabilities shall be maintained
- Alternate work sites shall be identified
- Essential personnel shall be defined
- Work-from-home procedures shall be documented

## 4. Roles & Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibilities |
|------|-----------------|
| Executive Sponsor | Champion BC program, authorize resources |
| BC Manager | Manage program, coordinate planning, oversee testing |
| Department Heads | Develop departmental plans, participate in exercises |
| IT/DR Team | Manage technology recovery, maintain DR capabilities |
| HR | Employee communication, alternate work arrangements |
| All Employees | Know their role in continuity plans, participate in training |

## 5. Compliance

### 5.1 Monitoring
- Plan completion and currency shall be tracked
- Test completion shall be monitored
- BIA updates shall be tracked
- Recovery capability shall be verified

### 5.2 Enforcement
- Departments without current plans shall be escalated
- Failed tests shall result in remediation requirements
- Critical gaps shall be reported to leadership

## 6. Exceptions

- Exceptions to testing requirements must be approved by BC Manager
- Exceptions shall be documented with compensating measures
- Exceptions shall be reviewed annually
