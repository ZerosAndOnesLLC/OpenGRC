## 1. Purpose

This Data Retention Policy establishes requirements for retaining and disposing of [Organization Name]'s data and records. This policy ensures that data is retained for required periods, disposed of appropriately, and that retention practices comply with legal, regulatory, and business requirements.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:
- All data and records regardless of format (electronic, paper, other media)
- All employees, contractors, and third parties managing organizational data
- All systems and storage locations containing organizational data
- Data created, received, or maintained in the course of business

## 3. Policy Statements

### 3.1 Retention Principles

#### 3.1.1 Core Principles
- Retain data only as long as legally required or business necessary
- Apply consistent retention periods across similar data types
- Dispose of data securely when retention period expires
- Document retention decisions and practices
- Suspend disposal during legal holds

#### 3.1.2 Retention Period Determination
Consider these factors when determining retention periods:
- Legal and regulatory requirements
- Contractual obligations
- Litigation and audit requirements
- Business operational needs
- Historical and archival value
- Privacy and data minimization principles

### 3.2 Standard Retention Periods

#### 3.2.1 Financial Records

| Record Type | Retention Period | Legal Basis |
|-------------|------------------|-------------|
| Tax returns and supporting documents | 7 years | IRS requirements |
| General ledger | 7 years | Tax/audit requirements |
| Accounts payable/receivable | 7 years | Tax requirements |
| Bank statements | 7 years | Tax requirements |
| Expense reports | 7 years | Tax requirements |
| Audit reports | 7 years | Audit requirements |
| Invoices | 7 years | Tax requirements |

#### 3.2.2 Human Resources Records

| Record Type | Retention Period | Legal Basis |
|-------------|------------------|-------------|
| Employee personnel files | 7 years after termination | Employment law |
| Payroll records | 7 years | IRS, FLSA |
| I-9 forms | 3 years or 1 year after termination | Immigration law |
| Benefits records | 6 years | ERISA |
| Job applications (not hired) | 2 years | EEOC guidelines |
| Performance reviews | 5 years after termination | Employment law |
| Training records | Duration of employment + 3 years | OSHA, compliance |

#### 3.2.3 Legal and Compliance Records

| Record Type | Retention Period | Legal Basis |
|-------------|------------------|-------------|
| Contracts | 7 years after expiration | Statute of limitations |
| Corporate records (bylaws, minutes) | Permanent | Corporate law |
| Insurance policies | Permanent | Legal protection |
| Litigation files | 7 years after resolution | Legal requirements |
| Compliance audit reports | 7 years | Regulatory requirements |
| Regulatory filings | 7 years minimum | Regulatory requirements |

#### 3.2.4 Customer and Business Records

| Record Type | Retention Period | Legal Basis |
|-------------|------------------|-------------|
| Customer contracts | 7 years after expiration | Contractual/legal |
| Customer communications | 3 years | Business need |
| Sales records | 7 years | Tax requirements |
| Marketing materials | 3 years after campaign | Business need |
| Vendor contracts | 7 years after expiration | Contractual/legal |

#### 3.2.5 IT and Security Records

| Record Type | Retention Period | Legal Basis |
|-------------|------------------|-------------|
| Security logs (access, authentication) | 1 year minimum | Compliance (SOC 2, PCI) |
| System logs | 90 days minimum | Operations/compliance |
| Security incident records | 7 years | Legal/compliance |
| Backup tapes | Per backup policy | Business continuity |
| Change management records | 3 years | Compliance |

#### 3.2.6 Personal Data (Privacy-Specific)

| Record Type | Retention Period | Legal Basis |
|-------------|------------------|-------------|
| Customer personal data | Duration of relationship + 3 years | Privacy regulations |
| Consent records | Duration of consent + 3 years | GDPR, CCPA |
| Data subject requests | 3 years | GDPR documentation |
| Marketing preferences | Until withdrawn + 2 years | Privacy regulations |

### 3.3 Legal Holds

#### 3.3.1 When Required
- Pending or threatened litigation
- Government investigation
- Regulatory inquiry
- Internal investigation
- Audit requirements

#### 3.3.2 Legal Hold Process
- Legal department initiates and manages holds
- Affected custodians shall be notified in writing
- Normal destruction shall be suspended for held data
- Hold scope and custodians shall be documented
- Holds shall be lifted when matter is resolved

### 3.4 Data Disposal

#### 3.4.1 Disposal Methods

| Data Type | Disposal Method |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Paper documents (confidential) | Cross-cut shredding |
| Paper documents (standard) | Recycling after shredding |
| Electronic files | Secure deletion (multiple overwrites) |
| Hard drives/SSDs | Degaussing or physical destruction |
| Backup tapes | Degaussing or physical destruction |
| Optical media | Physical destruction |

#### 3.4.2 Disposal Requirements
- Disposal shall render data unrecoverable
- Disposal shall be documented for sensitive data
- Certificates of destruction for Restricted data
- Third-party disposal vendors shall be vetted
- Disposal shall not occur during legal holds

### 3.5 Third-Party Data

- Contracts shall specify retention requirements
- Data return or destruction upon contract termination
- Certification of destruction may be required
- Cloud provider retention settings shall be configured appropriately

### 3.6 Email Retention

- Business email shall be retained for 3 years
- Litigation-relevant email subject to legal hold
- Personal email should be minimized in business systems
- Automated deletion after retention period

### 3.7 Exceptions

#### 3.7.1 Extended Retention
- Legal holds extend retention indefinitely until released
- Specific regulations may require longer retention
- Business needs may justify extended retention (with approval)

#### 3.7.2 Early Disposal
- Data may be disposed before standard period if:
  - No legal or regulatory requirement applies
  - Legal/compliance approval obtained
  - Documented justification provided

## 4. Roles & Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibilities |
|------|-----------------|
| Legal | Define retention periods, manage legal holds |
| Records Manager | Implement retention program, coordinate disposal |
| IT | Configure retention in systems, execute secure disposal |
| Department Heads | Ensure compliance within departments |
| All Employees | Follow retention schedules, comply with legal holds |

## 5. Compliance

### 5.1 Monitoring
- Retention schedule adherence shall be audited annually
- Legal holds shall be tracked and reviewed
- Disposal activities shall be logged
- Exceptions shall be documented

### 5.2 Enforcement
- Premature disposal during legal hold is prohibited
- Violations may result in disciplinary action
- Serious violations may have legal consequences

## 6. Exceptions

- Exceptions require Legal department approval
- Business justification must be documented
- Exceptions shall be reviewed annually
