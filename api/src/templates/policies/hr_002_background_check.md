## 1. Purpose

This Background Check Policy establishes requirements for conducting pre-employment and ongoing background screening at [Organization Name]. This policy ensures that individuals with access to sensitive information, systems, or facilities meet trustworthiness standards while complying with applicable laws.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:
- All candidates for employment (full-time, part-time)
- Contractors and consultants with access to sensitive resources
- Individuals in positions of trust or with elevated access
- Existing employees transferring to sensitive positions
- Third parties requiring physical or logical access

## 3. Policy Statements

### 3.1 Background Check Requirements

#### 3.1.1 Standard Background Checks
Required for all employees and applicable contractors:
- Identity verification
- Social Security Number verification
- Criminal history (7-year search)
- Employment verification (past 5 years)
- Education verification (highest degree)
- Reference checks

#### 3.1.2 Enhanced Background Checks
Required for positions with elevated access or trust:
- All standard checks plus:
- Credit history review
- Extended criminal history (where permitted)
- Professional license verification
- Sanctions and watchlist screening
- Motor vehicle records (if driving required)

#### 3.1.3 Position Categories

| Category | Description | Check Level |
|----------|-------------|-------------|
| Standard | General positions without sensitive access | Standard |
| Elevated | Access to confidential data or systems | Enhanced |
| Trusted | Financial, executive, or security roles | Enhanced + Credit |
| Regulated | Healthcare, financial services, etc. | Per regulatory requirement |

### 3.2 Timing of Background Checks

#### 3.2.1 Pre-Employment
- Conditional offers may be extended pending background check
- Background checks initiated after offer acceptance
- Start date contingent on satisfactory results
- Results typically received within 5-10 business days

#### 3.2.2 Periodic Rescreening
- Employees in trusted positions: Rescreened every 3 years
- Employees with regulatory requirements: Per regulation
- Upon promotion to sensitive position: Rescreening required
- Self-disclosure of relevant changes: Required

### 3.3 Consent and Disclosure

#### 3.3.1 Candidate Rights
- Written authorization required before checks
- Clear disclosure of what will be checked
- Copy of rights under Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA)
- Opportunity to explain or dispute findings
- Pre-adverse and adverse action notices if applicable

#### 3.3.2 Required Notices
- Disclosure that background check will be conducted
- Summary of rights under FCRA
- State-specific notices where required
- Clear description of check scope

### 3.4 Criminal History Considerations

#### 3.4.1 Evaluation Factors
Criminal history does not automatically disqualify candidates. Consider:
- Nature and gravity of the offense
- Time elapsed since offense
- Relationship to job responsibilities
- Evidence of rehabilitation
- Number and frequency of offenses

#### 3.4.2 Prohibited Considerations
In accordance with applicable law:
- Arrests without conviction (in most jurisdictions)
- Expunged or sealed records
- Juvenile records (in most cases)
- Old convictions beyond legal lookback period
- Records prohibited by state "ban the box" laws

### 3.5 Adverse Decisions

#### 3.5.1 Process
If background check results may lead to adverse action:
1. Provide pre-adverse action notice
2. Include copy of background report
3. Include FCRA summary of rights
4. Allow reasonable time to respond (typically 5 business days)
5. Consider candidate's response
6. If decision stands, provide final adverse action notice

#### 3.5.2 Documentation
- Document decision-making process
- Retain records per retention policy
- Ensure consistency in similar situations
- Legal review for sensitive decisions

### 3.6 Confidentiality

#### 3.6.1 Handling of Results
- Background check results are confidential
- Access limited to HR and hiring decision-makers
- Results stored securely with restricted access
- Results not shared with unauthorized parties

#### 3.6.2 Retention
- Background check results retained for term of employment plus 3 years
- Unsuccessful candidate records retained for 2 years
- Secure destruction after retention period

### 3.7 Vendor Management

#### 3.7.1 Background Check Vendor Requirements
- FCRA-compliant consumer reporting agency
- Appropriate certifications and accreditations
- Data protection and security standards
- Accuracy verification processes
- Dispute resolution procedures

#### 3.7.2 Ongoing Monitoring
- Regular vendor performance reviews
- Accuracy audits
- Compliance verification
- Service level monitoring

### 3.8 International Considerations

- International background checks where legally permitted
- Compliance with local data protection laws
- Country-specific search capabilities
- Extended timelines for international checks
- Cultural and legal differences considered

### 3.9 Contractor and Third-Party Screening

#### 3.9.1 Requirements
- Contractors with access to sensitive resources require background checks
- Verification that vendor has conducted appropriate screening
- Contractual requirements for background check standards
- Rescreening for long-term contractors

#### 3.9.2 Validation
- Attestation from vendor of screening completion
- Right to require specific checks for high-risk access
- Audit rights for verification

### 3.10 Self-Disclosure

#### 3.10.1 Ongoing Obligation
Employees must disclose within 5 business days:
- Criminal charges or convictions
- Loss of professional license
- Regulatory sanctions
- Other relevant changes in status

#### 3.10.2 Failure to Disclose
- Failure to disclose may result in disciplinary action
- Discovered unreported information reviewed on case basis
- Intentional concealment is serious violation

## 4. Roles & Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibilities |
|------|-----------------|
| HR | Conduct checks, evaluate results, maintain confidentiality |
| Hiring Managers | Identify position category, participate in decisions |
| Legal | Advise on compliance, review adverse decisions |
| Background Check Vendor | Conduct searches, ensure FCRA compliance |
| Candidates/Employees | Provide consent, disclose relevant information |

## 5. Compliance

### 5.1 Monitoring
- Check completion rates tracked
- Adverse decision consistency audited
- Vendor performance monitored
- Regulatory compliance verified

### 5.2 Enforcement
- Candidates with disqualifying findings not hired
- Employees failing rescreening reviewed case-by-case
- Failure to self-disclose may result in termination
- False statements on applications grounds for termination

## 6. Exceptions

- Exceptions require HR Director and Legal approval
- Emergency hires may start with checks pending (limited scenarios)
- Exceptions documented with risk assessment
