# Vendor Management Policy

**Policy Code:** COMP-002
**Version:** 1.0
**Effective Date:** [To be set]
**Review Date:** [Annual review recommended]
**Owner:** [To be assigned - typically Procurement Manager or CISO]
**Category:** Compliance

## 1. Purpose

This Vendor Management Policy establishes requirements for assessing, engaging, and managing third-party vendors at [Organization Name]. This policy ensures that vendors meet security and compliance requirements and that vendor relationships do not introduce unacceptable risk to the organization.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:
- All third-party vendors, suppliers, and service providers
- Cloud service providers and SaaS applications
- Contractors and consultants
- Business partners with access to organizational data or systems
- All personnel involved in vendor selection and management

## 3. Policy Statements

### 3.1 Vendor Classification

#### 3.1.1 Criticality Tiers

| Tier | Classification | Criteria | Assessment Requirements |
|------|---------------|----------|------------------------|
| 1 | Critical | Access to highly sensitive data, essential to operations | Full assessment, annual review |
| 2 | High | Access to confidential data, important to operations | Standard assessment, annual review |
| 3 | Medium | Limited data access, supports business functions | Abbreviated assessment, biennial review |
| 4 | Low | No data access, replaceable services | Basic due diligence |

#### 3.1.2 Classification Factors
- Type and volume of data accessed
- System access level
- Impact if vendor is unavailable
- Regulatory requirements
- Financial exposure

### 3.2 Vendor Due Diligence

#### 3.2.1 Pre-Engagement Assessment
- Security questionnaire (SIG or equivalent)
- Review of security certifications (SOC 2, ISO 27001)
- Financial stability assessment (for critical vendors)
- Reference checks (for new vendors)
- Legal/compliance review

#### 3.2.2 Assessment Areas
- Information security program
- Data protection and privacy
- Access controls
- Incident response capabilities
- Business continuity/disaster recovery
- Subcontractor management
- Compliance with applicable regulations

#### 3.2.3 Documentation Requirements
- Security assessment results
- Certification reports (SOC 2, ISO 27001)
- Insurance certificates
- Business continuity plans (critical vendors)
- Data processing agreements (where applicable)

### 3.3 Contractual Requirements

#### 3.3.1 Required Contract Terms
- Confidentiality and non-disclosure provisions
- Data protection requirements
- Security incident notification requirements (within 24-72 hours)
- Right to audit
- Compliance with applicable laws
- Subcontractor approval requirements
- Termination and transition provisions
- Data return and destruction requirements

#### 3.3.2 Service Level Agreements
- Availability requirements
- Performance metrics
- Support response times
- Escalation procedures
- Penalties for non-compliance

### 3.4 Ongoing Monitoring

#### 3.4.1 Periodic Reviews
- Critical vendors: Annual assessment
- High vendors: Annual assessment
- Medium vendors: Biennial assessment
- Low vendors: As needed

#### 3.4.2 Monitoring Activities
- Review updated certifications and reports
- Monitor vendor security incidents
- Track performance against SLAs
- Review contract compliance
- Assess continued business need

#### 3.4.3 Continuous Monitoring
- Monitor security rating services (where available)
- Track news and breach notifications
- Review vendor communications
- Monitor service availability

### 3.5 Risk Management

#### 3.5.1 Risk Assessment
- Vendor risks shall be documented in risk register
- Risk ratings shall be updated based on assessments
- High-risk findings shall require remediation plans
- Unacceptable risks may result in vendor termination

#### 3.5.2 Risk Mitigation
- Require remediation of identified gaps
- Implement compensating controls where needed
- Consider alternative vendors for high-risk situations
- Document risk acceptance for remaining risks

### 3.6 Vendor Access

#### 3.6.1 Access Provisioning
- Access shall follow least privilege principle
- Access requires documented approval
- Access shall be specific and time-limited where possible
- Access shall be logged and monitored

#### 3.6.2 Access Termination
- Access shall be revoked upon contract termination
- Access shall be reviewed upon personnel changes
- Confirmation of access removal shall be obtained

### 3.7 Data Protection

#### 3.7.1 Data Handling Requirements
- Data classification shall be communicated to vendors
- Data handling requirements shall be contractually specified
- Data location and transfers shall be documented
- Data encryption requirements shall be enforced

#### 3.7.2 Data Processing Agreements
- DPAs required for vendors processing personal data
- DPAs shall comply with GDPR and applicable laws
- Sub-processor lists shall be maintained current
- Cross-border transfer mechanisms shall be documented

### 3.8 Incident Management

- Vendors shall report security incidents promptly
- Incident response procedures shall be coordinated
- Vendor incidents shall be tracked in incident management
- Post-incident reviews shall include vendor participation

### 3.9 Offboarding

#### 3.9.1 Termination Procedures
- Provide appropriate notice per contract
- Revoke all access immediately upon termination
- Obtain return or certification of data destruction
- Retain documentation of offboarding
- Update vendor inventory

## 4. Roles & Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibilities |
|------|-----------------|
| Procurement | Lead vendor selection, manage contracts |
| IT Security | Assess vendor security, define requirements |
| Legal | Review contracts, ensure compliance |
| Business Owner | Define requirements, manage vendor relationship |
| Privacy Team | Review data protection requirements |
| Risk Management | Assess vendor risk, track in risk register |

## 5. Compliance

### 5.1 Monitoring
- Vendor inventory shall be maintained current
- Assessment completion shall be tracked
- Contract expiration shall be monitored
- SLA compliance shall be reported

### 5.2 Enforcement
- Vendors failing assessments may be suspended
- Contract violations shall be escalated
- Non-compliant vendors may be terminated

## 6. Exceptions

- Exceptions require documented business justification
- Compensating controls must be identified
- Exceptions require IT Security and Legal approval
- Exceptions shall be reviewed annually
